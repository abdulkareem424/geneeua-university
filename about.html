University Administration

  • University President: Chief executive and academic officer, representing the university in all official matters.
  • University Council: Chaired by the President, includes Vice Presidents and Deans of Colleges. Ensures smooth academic and administrative operations.
  • College Dean: Oversees college departments and makes decisions related to college management.
  • Assistant Dean: Assists the Dean in managing academic affairs, faculty performance, and administrative matters.
  • Department Head: Supervises faculty, assigns courses, and ensures student progress.

Student Guidelines

  • Neutrality and Inclusivity: The university promotes tolerance, respect, and open dialogue.
  • Order and Conduct: Students must adhere to university rules and respect academic freedom.
  • Freedom of Expression: Verbal abuse against any university community member may result in suspension.
  • Classroom Etiquette: Mobile phones must be turned off during lectures, and cameras should be off during online exams for diploma and bachelor's students.
  • Smoking Ban: Smoking is strictly prohibited within the university campus.

Admissions and Registration

  • Bachelor's Degree Eligibility: High school diploma or equivalent required.
  • Registration Process: Annual registration with administrative fee and first tuition installment.
  • Online Registration: Submit documents in PDF format, receive confirmation email.
  • University ID Card: Issued during the first semester and renewed annually.
  • Graduate Programs: Open registration throughout the academic year.
  • Graduate Admissions Testing: Written exam and oral interview required.
  • Re-registration: Required at the beginning of each academic year.
  • Diploma Duration: 1.5 years, focusing on essential skills and specialization.
  • Changing Specializations: Requires passing written and oral exams.
  • Withdrawal: No refund of fees or return of certificates.
  • Disciplinary Period: Students under disciplinary action are not eligible for academic statements.
  • Graduation Requirement: Degrees awarded only to students completing studies within specified timeframe.

Academic Information

  • Enrollment Period: First week of May to end of July for Diplomas and Bachelor's degrees.
  • Start of Classes: Third week of September for Diplomas and Bachelor's degrees.
  • Academic Year End: June for Diplomas and Bachelor's degrees.
  • Semester Duration: 3.5 months for Diplomas and Bachelor's degrees.
  • Program Durations:
    • Diploma: 1.5 years (2 years for students with special needs)
    • Bachelor's: 2 years
    • Master's: 1.5 years
    • Doctoral: 2 years (can be completed in 1.5 years)
  • Online and In-Person Instruction: Education conducted online using specialized platforms, particularly Zoom.
  • Attendance: Mandatory for students, with participation marks affecting overall evaluation.
  • Examinations: Schedule determined for each subject, with specific conduct rules.
  • Grading System: Includes grades from A to D with specific GPAs assigned.
  • Re-examination Policy: Available for students who fail to meet minimum grade requirements.
  • Appeal of Grades: Students may appeal within one week of receiving grades.

Financial Policies and Aid

  • Tuition and Fees: Must be paid as per university schedule.
  • Refund Policy: Provided according to university policy with specific deadlines and conditions.
  • Payment Installments: Available subject to university financial policies and deadlines.
  • Financial Aid: Available for eligible students, including scholarships and grants.
  • Late Payment Penalties: May be incurred as per university financial policies.
  • Financial Counseling: Offered to assist students with managing finances.

Scholarship Opportunities for Graduate Students

  • Teaching Undergraduate Courses: Available for enrolled graduate students with expertise in the subject.
  • Up to 20% Scholarship:
    • Teaching and Workshop Facilitation
    • Administrative Support
    • Marketing and Promotion

Application Requirements for Scholarships:

  • Resume/CV
  • Academic Transcripts
  • Teaching Statement or Letter of Intent
  • Letters of Recommendation
  • Course Proposal (for teaching opportunities)
